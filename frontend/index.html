    If your scan comes back positive, do not train. Ringworm spreads easily through skin-to-skin contact on the mat. Stay off the mats until fully cleared.
    What is ringworm?

    Ringworm (tinea corporis) is a fungal skin infection, not an actual worm. It is extremely common in contact sports like BJJ, wrestling, and MMA due to close skin contact and shared mats.

    Symptoms
    • Ring-shaped rash with raised, scaly edges
    • Red or pink coloring, sometimes darker in the center
    • Itching or burning sensation
    • Can appear anywhere on the body
    • May expand over several days if untreated
    What to do if positive
    • Stop training immediately
    • Apply an antifungal cream (clotrimazole or terbinafine)
    • Keep the area clean and dry
    • Wash all training gear and towels in hot water
    • Return to training only when fully cleared, usually 2 to 3 weeks
    • See a doctor if there is no improvement after 2 weeks
    Prevention
    • Shower immediately after training
    • Wear clean gear every session
    • Avoid sharing towels or equipment
    • Check yourself regularly, especially after training with new partners
